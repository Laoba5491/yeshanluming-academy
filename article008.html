Because her parents were old and physically weak, the whole family was often bullied by others. The local security chief, a petty tyrant who practically held the power of life and death over the common people, decided by force that he would marry Miss Gao. He even sent men to seize her, stuffed her into a bridal sedan chair, and planned to carry her home for the wedding. As fate would have it, this happened to be witnessed by Zhu Bajie, the former Heavenly Marshal Tianpeng, who had descended into the mortal world. Seeing injustice, he stepped in without hesitation. He beat the security chief’s hired thugs so badly that they all fled in terror. Then he rescued Miss Cuilan and personally escorted her home. After saving Miss Gao Cuilan, Zhu Bajie saw how extraordinarily beautiful she was, and mortal desires suddenly arose in his heart. He thought that if he could marry such a woman, then his coming down to the human world would not have been in vain. In order to win Miss Gao’s hand, Zhu Bajie used every transformation skill he possessed to turn himself into the appearance of an ordinary man. Though still somewhat rough and broad in build, he looked acceptable enough. After arriving at Gao Village, Zhu Bajie had nowhere else to go, so he accepted the invitation of the Gao family patriarch and stayed there. To win Miss Gao Cuilan’s affection, Zhu Bajie worked desperately hard. He labored for the family with all his strength every single day, sweating from morning to night. When Miss Gao’s mother saw this, she was delighted beyond words. She thought that if Bajie became their son-in-law, not only would the family never again lack labor for the fields, but with such a strong and powerful man in the house, and one who had also saved Cuilan’s life, nobody would dare bully them again. So she cautiously sounded out her daughter’s feelings. As for Miss Gao herself, she too had quietly fallen for Bajie. First, he had saved her life. Second, he worked so hard for the family. If she married him, not only would there be someone to manage the farm work, but she would no longer fear being bullied by others. Therefore, when her mother finally asked her directly, though too shy to say the words aloud, she still nodded in agreement. As for Old Master Gao, he had long since taken a liking to Bajie as well. He simply had not known what his daughter thought. So when his wife brought up the matter, he immediately agreed. The whole family adored Bajie, and wedding discussions began at once. Old Master Gao believed the matter should not be delayed and ought to proceed immediately. When he learned that Zhu Bajie had no relatives in the human world, he decided not even to ask for a bride price, and instead invited him to marry into the Gao family as a live-in son-in-law. To avoid complications from waiting too long, the wedding was held only three days after the arrangement was settled. A great crowd of neighbors and friends from the surrounding area came to attend. Zhu Bajie, dressed in bright wedding colors, married Cuilan in a proper ceremony, and afterward a grand feast was held. Inside, Zhu Bajie was overwhelmed with joy. Thinking back on his past, he remembered how he had once been Marshal Tianpeng in Heaven. All because he jokingly pinched the backside of one of the Queen Mother’s maidens, he had been punished and cast down into the mortal world, reincarnated into the body of a pig, becoming this ugly creature he now was. Yet today, he had married such a beautiful woman as Miss Gao. To him, that alone made this entire life worthwhile. As for whether he would ever return to Heaven again, his heart had already grown cold toward such thoughts. Living out his days here seemed far happier than life in Heaven. After the wedding, the eating and drinking began in earnest. Everyone came to toast the groom. Bajie was so overjoyed that he forgot about the magic disguise he was using to maintain his human appearance. Once drunk, he could no longer control it. And so, happiness turned suddenly into disaster. He drank himself completely senseless and collapsed onto the ground. Gradually, the face of a great pig began to emerge again, revealing his true form. The Gao family and all the guests, seeing the drunken Zhu Bajie sprawled on the ground in his hideous pig appearance, screamed in terror and fled. Cuilan herself was frightened out of her wits and hid inside her room, too afraid to come out. Only Old Master Gao remained calm and sensible. He told Madam Gao not to panic. Then he ordered the farmhands to tightly bind the unconscious Zhu Bajie with ropes and throw him into the woodshed, guarded by two strong men. The next morning, Zhu Bajie woke from his drunkenness. Seeing himself tied up, he immediately understood that everything was ruined. And indeed, it was too late. He confessed the entire truth to Old Master Gao. After learning what had really happened, the old master actually felt quite sympathetic toward Zhu Bajie. But his daughter was terrified now, and this marriage could no longer continue. So he took out one hundred taels of silver and gave them to Zhu Bajie, telling him to go make his own way in the world. As Zhu Bajie was leaving and about to step through the gates of the estate, Cuilan came to see him. She had already learned the truth. She brought him a pair of sturdy cloth shoes with double-layered soles for the road ahead. Zhu Bajie felt his heart ache. In the end, he hardened himself. Then he turned back once more and looked fiercely at Cuilan one last time before finally walking out of Gao Village forever. (The end)
