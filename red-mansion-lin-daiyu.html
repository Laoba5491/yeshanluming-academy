賈寶玉後來終於明白，大觀園裡那麼多女孩子，真正讓他心死的人，只有林黛玉。別人的死，讓他悲傷；只有黛玉的死，像一把刀，剜走了他的心， 直接把他的靈魂也一起帶走了。 他第一次見到林黛玉時，他還只是個孩子。那天賈母命人領著外孫女進來，眾人只見一個瘦弱的小姑娘，神情清冷，眉目如煙，站在屋中，好像帶著一身江南水氣，靈氣。寶玉抬頭看見她時，心裡忽然猛地一震，脫口便說：「這個妹妹我曾見過的。」 滿屋子的人都笑了，以為他又在犯癡。只有寶玉自己知道，那不是亂講， 也不是玩笑。他是真的覺得，自己早就認識她。後來很多年裡，寶玉常常想，也許有些人，本就是前世欠下的債，所以今生一見，便再也放不下。 林黛玉和別的女孩子不同。她不像寶釵那樣端莊穩重，也不像湘雲那樣爽朗豪氣，更不像襲人那樣柔順體貼。她敏感、多心，動不動便流淚。說話卻又十分尖刻，有時一句話便把人噎得無言已對。因此，許多人都覺得她很難相處，可寶玉偏偏最懂她。 因為只有寶玉知道，黛玉那些尖刻背後，藏著的其實是害怕。 她太沒有安全感了。

她母親早逝，父親後來也病死，孤零零一個人寄居賈府。雖然賈母疼她，可她心裡始終明白，自己不是真正賈家的人。她不像寶釵，有母親，兄長；不像湘雲，有叔叔，嬸嬸；甚至不像迎春，探春她們，有一個名正言順的身份。她什麼都沒有，她唯一真正擁有的，只有寶玉。 所以她才那樣在意他。 寶玉和別的丫頭多說幾句話，她便難過；寶玉對寶釵稍微親近一些，她便傷心；寶玉一時冷落了她，她甚至能獨自哭上一夜。許多人笑她小性兒，說她愛吃醋。可是寶玉後來才明白，一個把全部生命，都寄託在一個人身上的女子，怎麼可能不怕失去。 偏偏，寶玉又是最讓她不安的人。 寶玉其實是真愛黛玉的，這一點，整個《紅樓夢》裡，沒有人比他更真。他們一起讀《西廂記》，那時桃花開得正好，寶玉偷偷把《會真記》帶進園子，兩人坐在花陰下，一句一句地讀。讀到「你是個多愁多病身，我就是那傾國傾城貌」時，黛玉紅了臉，轉身便走。可是走到一半，又忍不住回頭。那一刻，其實兩人心裡都已經明白了。 後來黛玉葬花，更成了《紅樓夢》中最令人心碎的一幕。 她拿著花鋤，把滿地落花一瓣一瓣埋進土裡，邊埋邊吟：「花謝花飛花滿天，紅消香斷有誰憐？」旁人只當她傷春，只有寶玉知道，她哭的其實是自己。她知道自己也像那些花，開得再美，終究還是要被風雨摧折。 寶玉挨打那一次，黛玉哭得最厲害。賈政拿著板子往死裡打，整個賈府都嚇壞了。寶玉被打得皮開肉綻，昏死過去。那天晚上，黛玉躲在房裡，哭得眼睛都腫了。後來她去看寶玉，只見他滿身是傷，心疼得一句話都說不出來，只會掉眼淚。寶玉卻反過來安慰她，只輕輕說了一句：「你放心。」 就是這三個字，讓黛玉記了一輩子。因為她一直以為，寶玉是真會守著她的。 可是後來她才知道，在賈府這樣的地方，真心是最沒用的東西。 賈母年紀越來越大，王夫人開始真正掌權，薛姨媽與王家的勢力也越來越重。整個賈府，上上下下，都在談「金玉良緣」。寶釵有金鎖，寶玉有通靈寶玉，人人都說，這才是天作之合。只有寶玉和黛玉，還守著那個「木石前盟」。 可是木石之盟，終究敵不過金玉之說。 寶玉其實不是不想反抗。他摔玉、發脾氣、鬧絕食，拼命想告訴所有人，他不要什麼金玉良緣。可是他太軟弱了。他離不開賈府，也離不開賈母，更沒有真正對抗整個家族的能力。他始終像個孩子，而黛玉，卻早已被現實逼成了一個大人。 所以她越來越瘦，病也越來越重。 她其實早知道，自己活不久了，只是心裡始終還抱著最後一點希望。她相信寶玉懂她，相信寶玉不會負她。所以當那場「掉包計」真正開始時，她才會徹底崩潰。 那幾天，大觀園裡到處張燈結綵，人人都忙著辦喜事。只有瀟湘館，冷得像一座墳。黛玉病得已經起不了床，她躺在那裡，聽著外面隱隱約約的鼓樂聲，心裡其實已經明白了。只是她始終不願相信，寶玉真的會娶別人。 直到最後，她終於知道，那場婚禮，是真的。 據說那一夜，她讓紫鵑把自己多年寫下的詩稿全部拿來，一頁一頁，一首一首，全燒了。火光映著她蒼白的臉，也映著她滿眼的淚。紫鵑哭著去搶，她卻不讓。因為她知道，這些東西，留著也沒用了。那些她與寶玉一起讀過的詩、一起談過的夢、一起度過的歲月，如今，都該燒了。 火越燒越旺，而黛玉的生命，也像那火光一樣，一點一點暗了下去。 有人說，她最後還喊著：「寶玉，寶玉，你好……」可是後面的話，終究沒能說出口。 也許，她是想說「你好狠」；也許，她是想說「你好糊塗」；又或者，她其實什麼都不想責怪。因為她心裡一直知道，寶玉不是不愛她，他只是沒有能力保護她。 可是對林黛玉來說，這就已經夠了。 她一生那樣驕傲，寧可死，也不肯低頭。所以她最後，還是走了。 有人說她是病死，有人說她是淚盡而亡，也有人說，她其實是自己不想活了。寶玉後來卻覺得，這些都不重要了。因為黛玉真正死去的那一刻，是她終於明白，這個世界，容不下真正的感情。 寶玉後來知道黛玉死訊時，整個人都瘋了。他衝進靈前，哭得像個孩子。可是那時，一切都晚了。那個陪他讀《西廂》的人沒有了，陪他葬花的人沒有了，那個最懂他的人，也永遠沒有了。 從那一天起，賈寶玉其實也死了一半，只是別人看不出來而已。 Looking back years later, Jia Baoyu finally understood that among all the girls of the Grand View Garden, there was only one whose death truly destroyed his heart—Lin Daiyu. The deaths of the others filled him with sorrow. But Daiyu's death was like a knife that carved his heart away and carried off his very soul. The first time Baoyu met Lin Daiyu, he was still only a child. That day, Grandmother Jia asked someone to escort her granddaughter into the room. Everyone saw a frail little girl standing quietly before them. She carried an air of cool elegance; her delicate features were as misty as distant mountains, as though she had arrived wrapped in the gentle moisture and quiet spirit of Jiangnan itself. The moment Baoyu looked up and saw her, something within him trembled. Without thinking, he blurted out, "I've met this cousin before." The whole room burst into laughter, believing he was talking nonsense again. Only Baoyu himself knew he was neither speaking carelessly nor making a joke. He truly felt that he had known her long before that day. Years later, he often wondered whether some people were simply debts left over from a previous life—so that the moment they met again in this one, they could never be separated. Lin Daiyu was unlike any of the other girls. She was not as dignified and composed as Baochai, nor as openhearted and carefree as Xiangyun, nor as gentle and considerate as Xiren. She was sensitive, fragile, and quick to tears. Yet at the same time, her words could be astonishingly sharp, leaving others speechless with a single remark. Many people found her difficult to get along with. Yet Baoyu understood her better than anyone else. For only Baoyu knew that beneath Daiyu's sharp tongue lay something else entirely—fear. Lin Daiyu was unlike any of the other girls. She was not as dignified and composed as Baochai, nor as openhearted and carefree as Xiangyun, nor as gentle and considerate as Xiren. She was sensitive, fragile, and quick to tears. Yet at the same time, her words could be astonishingly sharp, leaving others speechless with a single remark. Many people found her difficult to get along with. Yet Baoyu understood her better than anyone else. For only Baoyu knew that beneath Daiyu's sharp tongue lay something else entirely—fear. She simply had no sense of security. Her mother had died when she was young, and later her father passed away as well. Alone in the world, she came to live in the Jia household as a dependent. Grandmother Jia loved her dearly, yet Daiyu always knew that she was never truly one of the Jia family. Unlike Baochai, she had a mother and an elder brother. Unlike Xiangyun, she had uncles and aunts. Even Yingchun and Tanchun possessed an unquestioned place within the family. Daiyu had none of these. The only person she truly possessed was Baoyu. That was why she cared so deeply about him. If Baoyu spent too much time talking with another maid, she would become unhappy. If he showed Baochai even the slightest extra attention, she would be heartbroken. If he neglected her for a single day, she could spend the entire night weeping alone. Many people laughed at her, calling her narrow-minded and jealous. But only later did Baoyu realize that a woman who had entrusted her entire life to a single person could hardly help fearing that she might lose him. And unfortunately, Baoyu himself was the very person who caused her the greatest insecurity. Baoyu truly loved Daiyu. Of that, there was no doubt. Nowhere in Dream of the Red Chamber was there a love more genuine than his. Together they read The Story of the Western Wing. Peach blossoms were in full bloom. Baoyu secretly carried a copy of The Romance of the Western Chamber into the garden, and beneath the flowering trees they read it together, line by line. When they came to the verse, "You are one burdened with sorrow and illness; I am the beauty who could overthrow kingdoms," Daiyu blushed deeply and turned away. Yet after taking only a few steps, she could not resist looking back. At that moment, both of them already understood what neither dared to say aloud. Later, Daiyu's burial of the fallen flowers became one of the most heartbreaking scenes in the entire novel. She held a flower hoe in her hands and buried the fallen blossoms, petal by petal, into the earth. As she buried them, she softly recited: "Flowers fade, flowers fly, filling the sky; when red fades and fragrance dies, who will pity them?" Others thought she was merely grieving over spring. Only Baoyu knew that what she was truly mourning was herself. She knew that she, too, was like those flowers: however beautifully she might bloom, in the end she would still be broken by wind and rain. When Baoyu was beaten, Daiyu wept more bitterly than anyone else. Jia Zheng struck him with the board as though he meant to beat him to death, terrifying the entire Jia household. Baoyu was beaten until his flesh was torn and he lost consciousness. That night, Daiyu hid in her room and cried until her eyes were swollen. Later, when she went to see Baoyu and saw his body covered with wounds, her heart ached so badly that she could not say a word. All she could do was weep. Baoyu, however, comforted her instead, saying only three soft words: "Don't worry." Those three words stayed with Daiyu for the rest of her life. Because she had always believed that Baoyu truly would remain by her side. But later she came to understand that in a place like the Jia household, sincerity was the most useless thing of all. Grandmother Jia grew older and older. Lady Wang began to hold real authority, and the influence of Aunt Xue and the Wang family grew stronger by the day. Throughout the Jia household, from top to bottom, everyone was speaking of the "gold and jade match." Baochai had her golden lock, Baoyu had his Magic Jade, and everyone said this was a marriage made by Heaven. Only Baoyu and Daiyu still clung to the old "wood and stone pledge." But in the end, the pledge of wood and stone could not defeat the doctrine of gold and jade. It was not that Baoyu did not want to resist. He smashed his jade, lost his temper, refused to eat, and tried desperately to tell everyone that he wanted no so-called gold and jade match. But he was too weak. He could not leave the Jia household, could not leave Grandmother Jia, and did not truly possess the strength to oppose the entire family. He remained, in the end, like a child. Daiyu, however, had already been forced by reality to become an adult. And so she grew thinner and thinner, and her illness became worse and worse. In truth, she had long known that she would not live much longer. Yet deep in her heart, she still held on to one last hope. She believed that Baoyu understood her. She believed that Baoyu would not betray her. That was why, when the "bride-switching scheme" finally began, she collapsed completely. During those days, the Grand View Garden was hung everywhere with lanterns and decorations. Everyone was busy preparing for the wedding. Only the Xiaoxiang Pavilion felt as cold as a tomb. Daiyu was already so ill that she could no longer rise from bed. She lay there, listening to the faint sound of music and drums drifting in from outside. In her heart, she already understood everything. She simply refused to believe that Baoyu would truly marry someone else. At last, she finally learned that the wedding was real. It is said that on that night, she asked Zijuan to bring out all the poems she had written over the years. Page by page, poem by poem, she burned them all. The flames shone upon her pale face and reflected the tears in her eyes. Zijuan cried and tried to snatch them away, but Daiyu would not allow it. She knew that those things no longer had any use. The poems she and Baoyu had once read together, the dreams they had once spoken of together, the years they had once shared together—now all of them had to be burned. The fire burned brighter and brighter, while Daiyu's life, like that fading flame, slowly dimmed away. Some people say that with her final breath, she still called out, "Baoyu... Baoyu... you..." But the words that were to follow never left her lips. Perhaps she meant to say, "You are so heartless." Perhaps she meant, "You are so foolish." Or perhaps she wished to blame him for nothing at all. Deep in her heart, she had always known that it was not because Baoyu did not love her. It was simply because he did not possess the strength to protect her. But for Lin Daiyu, that alone was enough. She had lived her entire life with pride. Rather than bow her head, she chose death. And so, in the end, she was gone. Some say she died of illness. Others say she wept until her tears were exhausted and her life ended. Still others believe that, in truth, she simply no longer wished to live. But later Baoyu came to feel that none of those explanations truly mattered. For the moment Daiyu really died was the moment she finally understood that this world could not make room for genuine love. When Baoyu finally learned of Daiyu's death, he completely lost himself. He rushed to her coffin and wept like a child. But by then, everything was already too late. The person who had read The Story of the Western Wing with him was gone. The one who had buried fallen flowers beside him was gone. The one who had understood him better than anyone else was gone forever. From that day onward, half of Jia Baoyu died as well，though no one else could see it. (To be continued)
