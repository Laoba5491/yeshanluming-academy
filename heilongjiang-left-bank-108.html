那一年冬天來得格外早，十月剛過，長白山已經接連下了幾場大雪，山林變得越來越寂靜。 青兒的小木屋四周，除了風聲和偶爾傳來的鳥鳴，幾乎再也聽不到別的聲音。 這天傍晚，青兒剛從山下回來，她今天走了很遠的路。去拜訪一位九十多歲的慘案倖存老人。 老人已經有些糊塗， 清醒時，還能正常交流， 糊塗時， 就什麼也說不清了。 不過， 不管清醒， 還是糊塗， 他翻來覆去都一直在說著同一句話： 「黑龍江裡的死人，多得能堵住江面。」 雖然今天很辛苦， 但是青兒還是很高興， 因為這位老人真的已經時日不多了， 但是能在最後一刻， 把他的回憶紀錄下來， 青兒感覺自己的辛苦值了。 等她回到木屋時，天已經完全黑了。她把背包放下，又往火坑裡添了幾塊松木柈子，火焰很快升了起來，屋裡漸漸暖和起來。 就在這時，她聽到外面好像傳來一陣奇怪的聲音，像是有人踏雪，又像是有什麼大的野獸經過。

Around Qing'er's little cabin, there was almost no sound except for the endless whisper of the wind and the occasional call of a bird. Late that afternoon, Qing'er had just returned from the foot of the mountain. She had walked a great distance that day to visit an elderly survivor of the massacre, a man already well into his nineties. The old man had begun to lose his clarity of mind. When he was lucid, he could still carry on a normal conversation. When confusion overtook him, however, nothing he said made much sense. To preserve his memories before they disappeared forever made every hardship worthwhile. By the time she returned to the cabin, darkness had completely fallen. She set down her backpack and added several pine logs to the fire pit. The flames quickly sprang to life, and warmth gradually spread through the little wooden house. Just then, she thought she heard a strange sound outside. It was as if someone were walking through the snow—or perhaps a large animal was passing nearby. Immediately alert, Qing'er became cautious. Living alone in the mountains had taught her that wolves and bears both roamed the forests of Changbai. During winter, when food was scarce, starving wolves could become bold enough to attack people. She could never afford to let down her guard. She reached for the hunting rifle leaning against the wall and slowly walked toward the door. Snow was still falling, while the bitter wind swayed the treetops without pause. She stood there listening carefully for a while. Then the sound disappeared. Just as she was about to step back inside and close the door, she suddenly caught sight of a dark figure moving slowly across the snowy field in the distance, heading toward the cabin. Qing'er's heart skipped a beat. Instinctively she raised her rifle. Looking more closely, however, she realized it was not a wolf. It was the shape of a person. The stranger also seemed to have spotted the cabin, for his pace noticeably quickened. The snowstorm grew heavier even as the distance between them steadily closed. At last, the figure came near enough for Qing'er to see clearly. She froze. At first she thought her eyes were deceiving her. She rubbed them, looked again, and stood there in complete astonishment. It was a familiar face. Though much thinner than before, she recognized him immediately. It was Lu Zhenggang. Separated by only a dozen paces, they stood facing one another in the snow, allowing the wind and storm to rage around them. Neither of them spoke. The mountain wind swept through the valley, lifting great clouds of snow that drifted between them. At that moment, time itself seemed to come to a halt. After a long silence, Qing'er finally whispered, almost as though speaking in a dream, "How... how could it be you?" Lu Zhenggang smiled. There was unmistakable weariness in his smile, but even more than that, there was the quiet relief of someone whose long search had finally come to an end. Brushing the snow from his shoulders, he said softly, "Why couldn't it be me? I finally found you..." Those few simple words brought tears instantly to Qing'er's eyes. For more than a year, all she had heard were cold words, suspicion, and rejection. Never once had anyone said to her, "I finally found you." Qing'er lowered her head. After a long moment, she finally asked, "How did you know I was here?" Lu Zhenggang smiled. "I searched..." "Searched?" "Mmm..." He nodded. "I started at your hometown. Then I found your village. After that I talked to the local hunters, then to the old people living at the foot of the mountain. I asked them one by one." Qing'er stood there speechless. She suddenly did not know what to say. She knew how difficult that search must have been, yet he spoke of it as though it were nothing more than an ordinary journey. "Some people said they had seen a young woman go into the mountains. Others said she was living in an old hunter's cabin. Still others said she carried a notebook everywhere and spent her days visiting elderly people." At that, he smiled again and said, "The moment I heard those stories, I knew it had to be you." At last Qing'er could no longer hold back. She smiled. Then, as she smiled, tears quietly rolled down her cheeks. "You fool," she whispered softly. "You're such a fool." Lu Zhenggang did not argue. He simply stood quietly in the snow, looking at her. After a while, Qing'er suddenly asked, "And... your mother?" This time, it was Lu Zhenggang who fell silent. The wind swept between them, stirring the hem of his coat. After a very long pause, he slowly spoke only two words. "She's gone." Qing'er understood immediately. She asked nothing more. She simply lowered her head. She knew that from this day forward, the man standing before her, just like herself, no longer had a home to return to. The firelight from inside the cabin spilled through the open doorway, stretching two long shadows across the snow. Qing'er looked at him. Then she quietly stepped aside and said softly, "It's cold outside. Come in." Lu Zhenggang did not enter immediately. He remained where he stood, looking at the humble little cabin, then at Qing'er, illuminated by the warm glow of the fire. At that moment, he suddenly felt that every hardship he had endured along the way had been worthwhile. He smiled. This time, he smiled like a man who had finally come home. (To be Continued)
