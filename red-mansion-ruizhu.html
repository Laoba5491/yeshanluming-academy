《紅樓夢》裡，有些人的死亡，曹雪芹用了整整幾回去鋪陳；有些人的死亡，卻只用了短短一兩句話。 然而，越是短，往往越令人不寒而慄。瑞珠，便是這樣一個人物。 她在《紅樓夢》中戲份極少，甚至許多讀者第一次讀完全書，都未必會特別注意這個名字。可是，只要稍微細想，就會發現，她的死，其實更加可怕。 因為她死得太快了。快得不像正常殉主，倒像一個忽然知道自己已經沒有活路的人，在最短時間內，替自己選擇了結局。 秦可卿死後，關於她的丫鬟瑞珠之死， 曹雪芹在原著第十三回裡，只淡淡寫了一句：「秦氏之丫鬟名喚瑞珠者，見秦氏死了，他也觸柱而亡。此事可罕，」 曹雪芹不愧是中國歷史上第一文學巨匠，他筆下的每一個字， 都有極深刻的寓意， 簡單四個字 「此事可罕」， 最後一個「罕」字， 給人留下無限想像空間。 就這麼簡單幾句，沒有鋪墊，沒有描寫，沒有交代。可偏偏是這種輕描淡寫，卻最讓人感覺毛骨悚然，脊背發冷。

In Dream of the Red Chamber, some deaths are developed over several entire chapters, while others are dismissed in no more than a sentence or two. Yet the shorter the account, the more chilling it often becomes. Ruizhu is one such figure. She plays only a tiny role in the novel. Many readers finish the book without paying much attention to her name. Yet anyone who pauses to think about her story soon realizes that her death may be one of the most terrifying of them all. Because she died far too quickly. Her death does not resemble the devoted suicide of a loyal servant following a beloved mistress. Instead, it feels like the desperate act of someone who suddenly realizes there is no road left but death—and chooses her own ending before anyone else can choose it for her. After Qin Keqing died, Cao Xueqin recorded the death of her maid Ruizhu in Chapter Thirteen with remarkable brevity: "Among Qin's maids was one named Ruizhu. Seeing that Qin had died, she dashed herself against a pillar and died as well. Such a thing was exceedingly rare." Cao Xueqin truly deserves his reputation as one of the greatest literary masters in Chinese history. Every word he chose carries layers of meaning. In this brief sentence, the four characters "此事可罕" ("Such a thing was exceedingly rare") are especially striking. The final character—"rare"—opens an endless space for the reader's imagination.

Seeing that Qin had died, she dashed herself against a pillar and died as well. Such a thing was exceedingly rare." Cao Xueqin truly deserves his reputation as one of the greatest literary masters in Chinese history. Every word he chose carries layers of meaning. In this brief sentence, the four characters "此事可罕" ("Such a thing was exceedingly rare") are especially striking. The final character—"rare"—opens an endless space for the reader's imagination. That is all he gives us. No preparation. No emotional description. No explanation. Yet it is precisely this understated treatment that sends a chill down the reader's spine. A servant who truly dies out of grief usually passes through stages of mourning，crying, keeping vigil beside the body, arranging the funeral, or lingering in unbearable sorrow. Ruizhu does none of these. Almost immediately after Qin Keqing dies, she throws herself against a pillar and kills herself. This does not look like grief overwhelming the heart. It looks far more like death born of terror. Or perhaps... a death that was forced upon her. Ruizhu was Qin Keqing's personal senior maid. Like the younger maid Baozhu, she had accompanied Qin Keqing from the Qin family into the Jia household. She knew every detail of her mistress's daily life. She knew whom Qin Keqing met. She likely knew what happened behind closed doors at night. And Qin Keqing herself is perhaps the most mysterious，and most ambiguous，young woman in Dream of the Red Chamber. Outwardly, she was Jia Rong's wife and the young mistress of the Ningguo Mansion. Yet throughout the entire Ningguo Mansion hangs an atmosphere that cannot quite be described，an invisible sense of corruption and unease. Most disturbing of all is the relationship between Qin Keqing and her father-in-law, Jia Zhen: a relationship never openly stated, yet never entirely concealed. It hovers like a mass of black smoke hidden deep within the mansion, casting its shadow over the entire household and giving off the stench of moral decay. Later, the commentator Zhiyanzhai left behind the famous annotation: "Qin Keqing met her disgraceful end in Tianxiang Tower." The phrase has long been understood as implying that her death was entwined with a sexual scandal rather than resulting from natural causes. Those few words almost tear away the final veil that Cao Xueqin deliberately left hanging. In other words, Qin Keqing's death was very likely far from innocent. And Ruizhu, as her personal maid, could hardly have remained ignorant of the truth behind her mistress's death. She may have stood guard outside Qin Keqing's chamber late at night. She may have seen Jia Zhen entering and leaving Tianxiang Tower. She may have overheard conversations never meant for her ears. She may have witnessed scenes that no one was ever supposed to see. She may well have been a direct witness to the truth behind Qin Keqing's death. She simply knew too much. And that is precisely what made her situation so terrifying. In a place like the Ningguo Mansion, knowing too much was itself a fatal danger. Especially for a woman. She possessed neither the protection of noble birth nor the power enjoyed by men. She was merely a maidservant，someone whose life could be erased at any moment without consequence. So when Qin Keqing suddenly died, Ruizhu may have understood one terrible truth immediately: She would be next. And so she chose the quickest solution. She died before anyone else could decide her fate. Or perhaps her death was never entirely her own choice at all.Perhaps it was, in the truest sense, a death arranged for her. Many readers describe the Ningguo Mansion as nothing more than a nest of lust and moral corruption. But that is not what is most frightening. Its deepest horror lies not merely in the masters' sexual depravity.It lies in the immeasurable darkness hidden beneath it all. Within that household, human life meant almost nothing，least of all the lives of servants. The masters could commit scandals, even crimes, and still find ways to smooth everything over with money and influence. But when the time came to bury the truth and preserve the family's reputation, it was always the weakest, the most powerless, and those who knew too much who had to die. Ruizhu was almost certainly one of them. Baozhu, the other maid, chose a different path in order to survive. After Qin Keqing's death, Baozhu voluntarily asked to become Qin Keqing's adopted daughter and remain beside her coffin as its guardian. On the surface, it appeared to be the highest expression of loyalty. But upon closer reflection, was it not also an act of self-preservation? She understood that only by wrapping herself in the protective cloak of "the faithful servant devoted to her mistress" might she have any hope of surviving. Ruizhu chose differently，She died immediately. One sought life. The other chose death. Yet both point unmistakably toward the same conclusion. Qin Keqing's death was almost certainly far more than a simple death from illness. There was very likely another truth hidden beneath the surface. Because the matter touched upon Jia Zhen's honor and reputation, anyone who knew what had really happened had to be silenced forever. Only then could the master feel secure. For the young Jia Baoyu, this was also a tremendous emotional shock. The relationship between Baoyu and Qin Keqing had always been an unusual one. His first true encounter with the mysteries of love and sexuality took place in Qin Keqing's own chamber. When he wandered through the Land of Illusion in his dream, the woman who shared the dream of love with him，known as Jianmei, "She Who Combined All Beauties"，also bore the childhood name Keqing. For Baoyu, Qin Keqing was never merely his cousin's wife. She was like a dream itself，a dream woven of tenderness, desire, illusion, and death. When that dream suddenly shattered, Ruizhu immediately followed by dying after dashing herself against a pillar. The shadow cast by these two deaths descended upon Baoyu one after another with overwhelming force. Cao Xueqin records Baoyu's reaction in the original novel: "Since Lin Daiyu had recently returned home, Baoyu had become lonely and withdrawn, finding little pleasure in the company of others. Each night he went to bed listlessly. Then, while still in his sleep, he heard someone announce Qin Keqing's death. He sprang up at once, feeling as though a knife had pierced his heart. With a cry, he suddenly spat out a mouthful of blood." How could a boy still little more than a child react so violently merely upon hearing news of someone's death? The answer reveals how profoundly Qin Keqing's death shook his heart. Baoyu may still have been too young to understand the filthy secrets hidden within the Ningguo Mansion. Yet instinctively, he sensed something was terribly wrong. He realized that the world before him was not nearly as clean or as innocent as it appeared. Some things can never be spoken aloud. Some secrets become a death sentence the moment they are known. Later, one after another, the young women of the Grand View Garden would all walk toward their own tragic destinies. But perhaps the first people who allowed Baoyu to smell the scent of death were Qin Keqing and Ruizhu. One met her disgraceful end in Tianxiang Tower. The other died after smashing herself against a pillar before the mourning hall. From that moment onward, the deaths of these two women became a dark shadow that slowly spread across the entire world of Dream of the Red Chamber. (To be continued)
